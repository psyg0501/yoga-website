Asanas

 

Asanas are body postures practiced in hatha yoga. The practice of asanas originally had a spiritual purpose - that of attaining samadhi, a state of meditative consciousness.

 

Asanas work by placing the physical body in positions that cultivate awareness, relaxation and concentration. They free up the spine, limbs, and breathing so that the practioner can spend extended periods of time in a seated position.

 

Regular and correct practice of asanas improves physical flexibility, strength, and balance and vitality while reducing stress and alleviating chronic medical conditions such as asthma, diabetes and lower back pain and neck pain. For the elderly, asanas improve health and quality of life by reducing sleep disturbances and hypertension.
